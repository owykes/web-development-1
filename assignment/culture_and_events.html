Culture and Events

Things to do

Orkney, across its several islands, offers a dynamic mix of nature, culture, history and adventure. Each isle offers something different, with only 20 islands being inhabited out of 70. You can go with the known tides or carve your own path out against Orkney's versatile scenary.

History and Heritage

Most recently at Scapa Flow during both World Wars, and all the way back to being part of the Norwegian crown during the Viking era. Orkney has always played a key part in history. And if you think its only predictable inhabitants of the island that left their mark, did you know there's a beautiful Italian chapel in St Mary's? It was constructed by italian prisoners of war on the island, during the second world war. Just proving that you can't take anything about Orkney for granted.

Local Traditions and Culture

Tradition here isn't just rediscovery its been passed down through generations, still part of everyday life for Orcadians. The yearly folk festival brings crowds from all over the island and further a field, usually held in May and has been going strong since the 80's.
